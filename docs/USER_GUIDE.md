## Getting Started

### Creating Your Account

1. **Visit** [nitor.io](https://nitor.io)
2. **Click** "Register" or "Sign Up"
3. **Choose your registration method:**
   - Email and password
   - Google OAuth
   - GitHub OAuth
   - LinkedIn OAuth
4. **Complete** your email verification (if using email registration)
5. **Begin** your onboarding process

### Account Setup

During onboarding, you'll be asked to:

1. **Choose your handle** (e.g., @janesmith)
   - Unique identifier for your profile
   - Between 3-30 characters
   - Letters, numbers, and underscores only

2. **Select your institution**
   - Current academic affiliation
   - Can be updated later

3. **Choose your discipline**
   - Primary field of research
   - Helps connect you with relevant colleagues

4. **Write a brief bio**
   - 150-250 characters
   - Describe your research interests

### First Steps

After onboarding:

- [✓] Complete your profile (Settings → Profile)
- [✓] Upload a professional photo
- [✓] Add your ORCID (if available)
- [✓] Build your CV
- [✓] Make your first post
- [✓] Follow researchers in your field

## Publishing Content

### Content Types

NITOR supports three main content types:

#### 1. Posts
**Quick updates and thoughts**
- 280 character limit (standard posts)
- Up to 5000 characters (long-form posts)
- LaTeX math support ($...$)
- Markdown formatting
- Tag with keywords

#### 2. Articles
**Full research articles**
- Title and abstract required
- Full-text content
- Keywords and tags
- Citation support
- LaTeX rendering

#### 3. Preprints
**Unpublished research**
- Similar to articles
- Marked as "preprint"
- Version tracking
- DOI assignment (coming soon)

### Creating Content

**Step by step:**

1. **Click** the "Compose" button
2. **Choose** content type
3. **Write** your content
   - Use Markdown for formatting
   - Include LaTeX for math: `$E=mc^2$`
   - Add relevant tags
4. **Set visibility:**
   - PUBLIC: Anyone can see
   - CONNECTIONS: Only connections
   - PRIVATE: Only you
5. **Preview** before publishing
6. **Publish** to your network

### LaTeX Support

NITOR supports LaTeX mathematical expressions:

**Inline math:**
```
The equation $E = mc^2$ demonstrates...
```

**Display math:**
```
$$
\int_0^\infty e^{-x^2} dx = \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2}
$$
```

### Best Practices

[✓] **DO:**
- Write clear, concise posts
- Use proper citations
- Tag content appropriately
- Engage constructively
- Maintain professional tone

[✗] **DON'T:**
- Plagiarize or misrepresent work
- Share unpublished data without permission
- Make personal attacks
- Spam or self-promote excessively
- Violate copyright

## Building Your Network

### Connections vs. Followers

**Connections:**
- Mutual professional relationships
- Both parties must agree
- Can see each other's connection-only content
- Appear in each other's networks

**Followers:**
- One-way relationship
- No approval needed
- See your public content
- Don't automatically see private content

### Finding Colleagues

**Search by:**
- Name or handle
- Institution
- Discipline
- Research keywords
- ORCID

**Discovery features:**
- Recommended connections
- People in your field
- Colleagues at your institution
- Authors you've cited

### Connection Etiquette

**When sending connection requests:**
- Include a personal message
- Mention common interests
- Explain why you want to connect
- Be professional and respectful

**Example:**
```
Hi Dr. Smith,

I'm a postdoc at MIT working on similar neural
network optimization problems. I've read your recent
paper on gradient descent methods and would love to
connect and discuss potential collaborations.

Best regards,
Jane
```

### Managing Your Network

**You can:**
- Accept/reject connection requests
- Remove connections
- Block users if necessary
- Mute specific users
- Report inappropriate behavior

**Enable 2FA:**
1. Settings → Security
2. Click "Enable 2FA"
3. Scan QR code with authenticator app
4. Enter verification code
5. Save backup codes (important!)
6. 2FA is now active

**Recommended apps:**
- Google Authenticator
- Authy
- Microsoft Authenticator

**Control your visibility:**

**Profile Privacy:**

- Who can contact you
- Who can see your connections

**Content Privacy:**
- Default visibility for posts
- Who can comment
- Who can repost

**Notification Privacy:**
- Email notifications
- Push notifications

NITOR protects your data:

**If you lose access:**

**If you have 2FA:**
- Use backup codes

## FAQs

### Account Questions

**Q: How do I change my handle?**
A: Settings → Profile → Edit Handle. Available once every 90 days.

**Q: Can I have multiple accounts?**
A: No, one account per person maintains community integrity.

**Q: How do I delete my account?**
A: Settings → Account → Delete Account. This is permanent.

### Content Questions

**Q: How do I cite someone else's work?**
A: Use standard academic citation format. NITOR auto-detects DOIs.

**Q: Can I edit after publishing?**
A: Yes, within 24 hours. Edits are marked and timestamped.

**Q: What happens to deleted content?**
A: Permanently removed after 30 days. Can be restored within 30 days.

### Privacy Questions

**Q: Who owns my content?**
A: You do. NITOR has non-exclusive license to display it.

**Q: Can I make my profile completely private?**
A: Yes. Settings → Privacy → Set all to Private.

**Q: How is my data protected?**
A: Industry-standard encryption, regular audits, GDPR compliance.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Which browsers are supported?**
A: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge (latest 2 versions).

**Q: Is there a mobile app?**
A: Web app works on mobile. Native apps coming Q2 2025.

**Q: Can I import publications from ORCID?**
A: Yes. CV → Publications → Import from ORCID.

### Billing Questions

**Q: Can I cancel Premium anytime?**
A: Yes, no cancellation fees. Features remain until period ends.

**Q: Do you offer student discounts?**
A: Yes! 50% off with valid .edu email.

**Q: What payment methods do you accept?**
A: Credit/debit cards, PayPal (coming soon).
